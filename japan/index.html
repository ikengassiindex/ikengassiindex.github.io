A composite resilience score for Japan's electrical grid — 6,003 substations across 47 prefectures and 10 EPCO territories — spanning a unique 50 Hz/60 Hz dual-frequency grid — 95 variables, 35 public data sources, zero proprietary dependencies.

Operated as a non-profit through a dedicated foundation — ensuring the Index serves the public interest, not commercial agendas.
Why Japan?

Japan operates one of the world's most reliable yet hazard-exposed transmission grids. The unique 50 Hz/60 Hz boundary (Itoigawa–Shizuoka Tectonic Line) creates structural vulnerability at three converter stations linking eastern and western networks. Combined with extreme seismic exposure (Nankai Trough, active fault networks), the world's most rapidly ageing population, and a super-ageing society driving infrastructure workforce decline, Japan presents an unparalleled case study in grid resilience under compounding systemic pressures. The SSI captures these interlocking risks through 95 calibrated variables drawn from 35 verified Japanese public sources — NIED, JMA, OCCTO, SBJ, METI, and all 10 EPCOs.
