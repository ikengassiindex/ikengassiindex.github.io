Section 01

Screening Objective & Design Philosophy

This methodology describes a multi-layer screening process to identify Italian MV distribution substations (cabine primarie) suitable for connecting a 9.9 MWn grid-scale Battery Energy Storage System (BESS). The 9.9 MW threshold sits just below Italy's 10 MW regulatory boundary for simplified connection procedures under CEI 0-16.

Design Principles

Conservative-progressive funnel. Hard filters (Steps 1–9) eliminate substations that are clearly unsuitable on engineering, environmental, or geographic grounds. Soft flags (grid congestion) identify risk factors without eliminating — these inform due diligence at the site shortlisting stage. The philosophy is: eliminate what is impossible, flag what is risky, rank what remains.

Open data only. Every dataset used in this methodology is publicly available at no cost. This ensures reproducibility and independence from proprietary DSO data. Where DSO data would be superior (e.g., actual transformer ratings, real-time load measurements), we note the gap and use the best available open proxy.

Living document. This methodology is versioned and designed to grow. New filters, enrichments, and data sources will be added over time as the project progresses from screening to site shortlisting to connection application.

Regulatory context: Italy's electricity distribution is governed by ARERA (Autorità di Regolazione per Energia Reti e Ambiente). Connection procedures for generation >10 MW require a full TICA process; below 10 MW, a simplified Econnextion procedure applies. Our 9.9 MWn target is deliberately sized to benefit from the simplified regime.

Section 03

Step 1 — Universe Definition

Objective

Define the universe of candidate substations: all Italian distribution-type substations (cabine primarie) from OpenStreetMap, augmented with GSE (Gestore dei Servizi Energetici) cabine primarie data.

Inclusion Rule substation.type = 'distribution' AND substation.country = 'IT' Excludes: minor_distribution (25,937 cabine secondarie), transmission (Terna RTN), industrial, traction, generation, converter substations.
Augmented with: 96 GSE-identified substations not present in OSM (see Section 12).

Rationale

Distribution substations are the natural connection point for MV-connected BESS. They receive HV power from Terna's transmission grid (typically 132–150 kV) and step it down to MV (10–20 kV). In Italy, these are the cabine primarie, operated primarily by e-distribuzione S.p.A. (~76%) and local distributors.

Minor distribution substations (cabine secondarie) are excluded because: (a) they operate at LV (400V), too low for a 9.9 MW connection; (b) their transformer capacity is typically 160–630 kVA, far below 10 MVA; (c) they have no spare HV/MV switchgear bays.

Section 05

Step 3 — Electrical Headroom Assessment

Objective

Estimate whether each substation has ≥10 MVA of spare firm capacity (on N-1 basis) to accommodate a 9.9 MW BESS connection without triggering overload.

Headroom Calculation headroom_MVA = firm_capacity_MVA − estimated_load_MVA firm_capacity  = (n_transformers − 1) × rating_per_tx
estimated_load = CS_count × 150 kW / (0.9 PF × 1000)
pass           = headroom_MVA ≥ 10

CS_count: cabine secondarie assigned via Voronoi nearest-neighbour to each CP.
150 kW per CS: ~70 households × 2.5 kW peak × 0.7 diversity factor.
N-1: loss of largest single transformer — standard CEI 0-16 planning criterion.

Data Sources

InputSourceMethod
Transformer count & ratingOSM + category-based estimatesOSM actual where mapped; ENEL standard assumptions by category
Downstream CS countOSM minor_distribution (25,937)Voronoi spatial assignment to nearest CP
Peak load per CSENEL engineering practice150 kW (250 kVA transformer at 60% utilisation)

Result

1,924 substations pass (≥10 MVA headroom). All 439 small substations eliminated — with a single transformer, they have zero firm capacity on N-1 basis. This is the correct engineering conclusion: a 9.9 MW BESS cannot safely connect to a single-transformer substation.

Step 4 — Generation Saturation [Informative]

Objective

Assess whether existing renewable generation already connected to a substation has consumed the available reverse-flow capacity, leaving insufficient room for an additional BESS export.

Saturation Model net_headroom_MVA = headroom_MVA − existing_generation_MW existing_gen = Σ (solar_MW + wind_MW) assigned to this CP
saturation_ratio = existing_gen / headroom_MVA
BESS_eligible = (net_headroom ≥ 10) AND (space_pass = true)

Renewable plants assigned to nearest CP by spatial proximity.
Solar default: 2 MW per plant (where OSM capacity tag missing).
Wind default: 10 MW per plant (where OSM capacity tag missing).
Only 72 of 5,653 plants have explicit OSM capacity tags.

Section 08

Step 5 — Flood Zone Exclusion

Objective

Exclude substations located in high-probability flood zones that pose unacceptable risk to a 25+ year BESS asset.

Decision Rule

Flood Filter Logic IF centroid ∈ P3_polygon → EXCLUDE (floodRisk = 'high') IF centroid ∈ P2_polygon AND ∉ P3 → FLAG (floodRisk = 'moderate')
ELSE → PASS (floodRisk = 'none')

P3 (elevata): 20–50 year return period. Unacceptable for BESS.
P2 (media): 100–200 year return. Flagged for site-specific assessment.
P1 (bassa): >200 year return. Acceptable residual risk.

Data Source

SourceDatasetVintageProjectionSize
ISPRA (Istituto Superiore per la Protezione e la Ricerca Ambientale)Mosaicatura Nazionale Pericolosità Idraulica v5.02020ETRS89 LAEA (EPSG:3035)P3: 28,791 polygons (328 MB); P2: 29,493 polygons (369 MB)
Why ISPRA mosaic as single source: The national mosaic harmonises flood hazard maps from all 7 District Basin Authorities into a consistent classification, reconciling PAI (older Law 183/89 maps) and PGRA (EU Floods Directive 2007/60/EC maps). Using a single authoritative source avoids conflicts and vintage discrepancies that arise from cross-referencing multiple regional datasets at the screening stage.

Method

GeoPandas spatial join of substation centroid points (reprojected to EPSG:3035) against P3 and P2 polygon shapefiles using R-tree spatial indexing.

Step 7 — Terrain Slope Analysis

Objective

Filter candidate parcels by terrain slope. BESS installations require relatively flat ground (≤6% slope) to minimise grading costs and ensure safe, stable foundations for containerised battery modules.

Data Source

SourceDatasetResolutionAccess
ESA / AirbusCopernicus DEM GLO-30 (TanDEM-X derived)30m (~1 arc-second)Free / AWS S3 public bucket

Method

For each of the 59,498 candidate parcels identified in Step 6, terrain slope was computed from the Copernicus DEM using Horn's 3×3 kernel (Sobel operator) for gradient estimation. Eight directional slopes were computed along compass bearings (N, NE, E, SE, S, SW, W, NW) plus an omnidirectional aggregate. The best (flattest) orientation is recorded for each parcel.

Slope Computation slope_omni = √(dz/dx² + dz/dy²) × 100  [%] dz/dx, dz/dy: Horn's method (3×3 Sobel kernel) on DEM elevation grid
slope_dir(θ) = |dz/dx · sin(θ) + dz/dy · cos(θ)| × 100
pass = mean_omnidirectional_slope ≤ 6%

DEM tiles: Cloud-Optimized GeoTIFF, 3600×3600 pixels, float32, EPSG:4326.
67 tiles covering Italian parcel locations (~1.2 GB total).
Small parcels (<3 DEM pixels): centroid + 3×3 neighbourhood fallback.

Step 8 — Evacuation Route Assessment

Objective

Verify that each candidate parcel has a viable cable evacuation route to its nearest substation, routed along public roads and paths, with total distance ≤450 metres. This is the final hard elimination step.

Data Source

SourceDatasetVintageAccess
OpenStreetMapHighway ways (Overpass API)March 2026 (live)Free / ODbL

Method

Evacuation Route Logic total_distance = snap_parcel + network_path + snap_substation 1. Query Overpass API for highway ways within ~8.9 km tiles (16 highway types, excluding private access)
2. Build undirected weighted graph (NetworkX): nodes = OSM road nodes, edges = Haversine-weighted segments
3. Snap parcel centroid and substation to nearest graph nodes (max 100m snap distance)
4. Dijkstra shortest path between snap points
5. pass = total_distance ≤ 450m

~930 Overpass tiles cached for all of Italy. Grid-cell spatial indexing (~220m cells) for fast nearest-node lookup.
If snap distance >100m for either endpoint, parcel fails (too far from road network).

Objective

Assess each candidate parcel against Italian DSP (Dichiarazione di Sostenibilità Paesaggistica) buffer zone requirements. A BESS site must maintain minimum distances from residential buildings, roads, railways, and power infrastructure to comply with acoustic and safety regulations.

Analysis Method

For each candidate parcel (those passing Steps 1–8 with slope ≤ 6% and evacuation route ≤ 450m), we query the OpenStreetMap Overpass API to find nearby buildings, roads, railways, and power lines within a search radius of 500m. The Haversine formula computes exact distances from each parcel centroid to the nearest feature in each category.

Acoustic Classification acoustic(p) = { suitable if dbld > 300m, conditional if 150m ≤ dbld ≤ 300m, not_suitable if dbld < 150m } where dbld = min distance from parcel centroid to nearest building (OSM building=*)
Infrastructure Compliance infra_ok(p) = NOT(road_violation OR rail_violation OR power_violation) where each violation = dfeature < buffertype for the nearest feature of that type

Section 11c

Cadastral Document Harvesting

Objective

Systematically download official cadastral documents for candidate parcels from the Italian Catasto via the OpenAPI platform. These documents enrich the BESS site assessment with ownership data (visura catastale), boundary maps (estratto mappa), and building floor plans (elaborato planimetrico).

Document Types

DocumentContentUse in BESS Assessment
Visura CatastaleOwnership, land category, cadastral income (rendita)Land acquisition strategy, owner identification
Estratto MappaOfficial cadastral boundary map with parcel outlinesAccurate parcel geometry, boundary verification
Elaborato PlanimetricoBuilding floor plans and technical drawingsIdentify existing structures, building constraints

Pipeline Architecture

The harvester (catasto_harvester.py) uses an asynchronous POST → poll → GET workflow against the OpenAPI Catasto endpoints. Each cadastral reference is parsed into its constituent parts (provincia, comune, foglio, particella) from the NATIONALCADASTRALREFERENCE format used in Step 6.

Cadastral Reference Parsing A263_002000.41 → provincia=CS, comune=A263, foglio=2000, particella=41 where provincia is resolved from the ISTAT comune code via a lookup table

The pipeline includes state management (JSON checkpoint files per endpoint), daily request limits, consecutive error breaker (stops after 5 sequential failures), and resume capability for interrupted runs.

Methodological Assumptions

AssumptionValueSensitivity

Evacuation route threshold450 mConservative; longer runs possible with higher cost
Contiguity threshold (bundles)3 m vertex distanceAccounts for coordinate precision and minor gaps

Infrastructure buffer distances20–60 m roads, 30 m rail, 3–25 m powerBased on DSP / D.Lgs. 285/1992, DPR 753/1980, CEI 11-4

What This Methodology Does Not Cover

This screening explicitly does not address: actual transformer nameplate data (requires DSO disclosure), real-time load measurements, DNO connection queue status, detailed geotechnical or planning constraints, grid reinforcement costs, BESS commercial viability, environmental impact assessment, land-use zoning classification, or road access for heavy transport (transformer delivery). These are deferred to the site shortlisting and connection application phases.
